### The Problem with Traditional 2FA

To understand the necessity of Proton Authenticator, one must first understand the flaws in other methods. Many services offer 2FA via SMS text messages. While better than nothing, SMS authentication leaves you vulnerable to "SIM swapping" attacks, where hackers trick mobile carriers into transferring your phone number to a device they control 2\. Once they have your number, they receive your 2FA codes, bypassing your security entirely.  
Authenticator apps solve this by generating Time-based One-Time Passwords (TOTP) locally on your device 2, 3\. These six-digit codes expire every 30 seconds, making them significantly harder for attackers to intercept or crack 2\. However, many popular authenticator apps are proprietary, meaning you cannot verify how they handle your data, or they lack the ability to sync securely across devices, leaving you locked out if you lose your phone.
