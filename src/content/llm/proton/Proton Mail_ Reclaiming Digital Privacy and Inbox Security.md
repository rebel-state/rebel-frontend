### Privacy by Default, Not an Option

The core difference between Proton Mail and legacy providers like Gmail or Outlook is the architecture of the system. Proton uses **end-to-end encryption (E2EE)** and **zero-access encryption** by default 3\.  
This means that when you send an email to another Proton user, the message is encrypted on your device and can only be decrypted by the recipient. Not even Proton can read your emails 4\. This stands in stark contrast to providers that retain the keys to your data, allowing them to scan your messages for keywords to serve you targeted ads or train their AI models 3\. With Proton, your data belongs to you—not algorithms, advertisers, or third parties 5\.  
Because Proton is based in Switzerland, all user data is protected by some of the world’s strictest privacy laws 6\. This legal framework, combined with ironclad encryption, ensures that your private information remains truly private.

### A Fortress Against Cyber Threats

Switching to Proton Mail isn’t just about stopping corporate surveillance; it’s about upgrading your security posture against malicious actors. Email is often the primary entry point for cyberattacks, but Proton has built-in defenses to keep you safe.

* **Phishing Protection:** Proton Mail includes robust tools to verify email addresses and protect your inbox from scams and "phishing" attempts, where attackers try to steal your credentials 7, 8\.  
* **Tracking Protection:** Marketing emails often contain hidden "pixels" that track when you open an email, where you are located, and what device you are using. Proton Mail blocks these trackers automatically, allowing you to read your email without broadcasting your activity to marketers 9\.  
* **Dark Web Monitoring:** For those on paid plans, Proton offers Dark Web Monitoring to notify you if your email credentials have been exposed in third-party data breaches, allowing you to take action before hackers can access your accounts 10\.
