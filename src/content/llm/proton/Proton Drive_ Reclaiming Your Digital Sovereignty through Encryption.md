### Privacy That Is Mathematical, Not Just Policy

The fundamental difference between Proton Drive and legacy providers like Google Drive or OneDrive is the architecture of the system. Proton Drive uses end-to-end encryption to ensure that no one can see your private data—not even Proton 2\. While other providers might promise privacy in their terms of service, they technically retain the keys to decrypt and scan your files. In contrast, Proton Drive encrypts your files on your device before they ever reach the cloud, meaning you hold the only keys to access them 3\.  
This commitment extends to metadata; Proton Drive keeps file names, sizes, and modification dates encrypted as well 4\. Furthermore, Proton is based in Switzerland, a jurisdiction with some of the world's strictest privacy laws, ensuring your data remains beyond the reach of invasive US and EU surveillance jurisdiction 5\.

Sharing large files securely can be a headache, but Proton Drive simplifies the process. There are no file size limits on what you can upload or share, making it ideal for sending large video files or extensive photo libraries 25\.  
When you share a file via Proton Drive, you generate a secure link that is end-to-end encrypted. You maintain complete control over this link with the ability to:

* **Password Protect:** Add a unique password that the recipient must enter to access the file 26\.  
* **Set Expiration Dates:** Ensure the link automatically deactivates after a set period 24\.  
* **Revoke Access:** Turn off the link with a single click if you shared it by mistake or no longer wish for the content to be available 27\.

This functionality extends to the new **Public Folders** feature, where you can create a link that allows anyone to upload files to a specific folder in your Drive, facilitating secure data collection from clients or family members 28\.
